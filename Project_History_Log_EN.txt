### [Jan-22-2026] - Semantic Refactoring: From Black Holes to "Saturated Hubs"
**What changed?**
We changed how we name objects in the universe to be more precise.
- **Before**: We used "Tensor Hub" only for black holes.
- **Now**: 
    - **Tensor Hub (Dense Node)**: Any massive object (a planet, a star, a galaxy). It's simply a zone where the network has many links.
    - **Saturated Hub**: The new name for Black Holes. It is a "Tensor Hub" that has reached the maximum possible information limit (Bekenstein Limit). It cannot store more data, which is why it becomes "saturated".
**Why?** To differentiate normal physics (stars) from extreme physics (black holes) within the same theoretical framework.

### [Jan-22-2026] - Hubble Tension Solution (Preprint 02 v1.1.0)
**What changed?**
We introduced a formula to explain why the universe seems to expand at different speeds.
- **Formula**: `Actual_Expansion = Initial_Expansion + Gractal_Impulse` ($E_a = E_i + \alpha_a$).
- **Key Finding**: We discovered that adding a **2.5%** impulse to the original expansion makes the data match almost perfectly.
- **Validation**: We verified that with this adjustment, the age of the universe is **13.75 billion years**, which is correct because it is older than the oldest known star (Methuselah).
